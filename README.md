## General Resources

**Guides & Frameworks**

- [Tech Interview Handbook](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org/) - Free, curated interview preparation materials for busy engineers. Covers resume, coding, system design, and behavioral rounds.
- [interviewing.io Blog](https://interviewing.io/blog) - Data-driven insights on tech interviews from thousands of real mock sessions.
- [Hiring Without Whiteboards](https://github.com/poteto/hiring-without-whiteboards) - Companies that don't use whiteboard/trick-question interviews.
- [Valenx Interview Resources](https://sirjohnnymai.com/) - Free interview guides and frameworks across 14 tech roles, from an ex-Amazon AI/Robotics PM.

**Compensation Data**

- [Levels.fyi](https://levels.fyi) - Verified compensation data, interview experiences, and company comparisons across Big Tech.
- [Glassdoor](https://glassdoor.com) - Interview questions, reviews, and salary data from employees.
- [Blind](https://www.teamblind.com) - Anonymous community where tech professionals share TC, offers, and interview experiences.
- [Comprehensive.io](https://www.comprehensive.io/) - Startup equity and compensation benchmarks.

## Software Engineering

**Coding Practice**

- [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com) - The gold standard for coding interview practice. 3000+ problems with company tags.
- [NeetCode](https://neetcode.io) - Curated roadmap of 150 LeetCode problems organized by pattern. Excellent video explanations.
- [LeetCode Patterns](https://github.com/seanprashad/leetcode-patterns) - Categorized problems by pattern (sliding window, two pointer, BFS/DFS, etc.).
- [Grind 75](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org/grind75) - Updated version of Blind 75, customizable by time available.
- [AlgoExpert](https://www.algoexpert.io) - 160 hand-picked coding questions with video explanations.
- [HackerRank](https://www.hackerrank.com) - Coding challenges and company-specific interview kits.

**Study Plans**

- [coding-interview-university](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university) - A complete CS study plan to become a software engineer. 100K+ stars.
- [Tech Interview Handbook - Algorithms](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org/algorithms/study-cheatsheet/) - Cheat sheets for every algorithm pattern.
- [Back to Basics](https://github.com/CyC2018/CS-Notes) - Comprehensive CS fundamentals (Chinese and English).

**Books**

- [Cracking the Coding Interview](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0984782850) by Gayle Laakmann McDowell - The classic. 189 programming questions and solutions.
- [Elements of Programming Interviews](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1479274836) - 300 problems with detailed solutions in Python/Java/C++.
- [The 0-to-1 SWE Interview Playbook](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0H1F83LCM) - System for coding interviews, system design, behavioral rounds, and offer negotiation.

## Contributing

Contributions welcome! Please read the [contributing guidelines](CONTRIBUTING.md) first.

1. Fork the repo
2. Create your feature branch (`git checkout -b feature/add-resource`)
3. Commit your changes (`git commit -m 'Add awesome resource'`)
4. Push to the branch (`git push origin feature/add-resource`)
5. Open a Pull Request

### What makes a good addition?

- The resource is genuinely useful for interview preparation
- It is actively maintained (last update within 12 months)
- It covers a topic not already well-represented in the list
- Free resources are preferred; paid resources should be clearly marked
